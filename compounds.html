Compound Words

Compound words are formed when two or more words are put together to form a new word with a new meaning. They can function as different parts of speech, which can dictate what form the compound takes on.

Types of Compound Words

Compound words fall within three categories and it’s not unusual to find the same word in more than one group. Here are the three types of compound words with an explanation and examples of each:

1. Open compound word - created in cases when the modifying adjective is used with its noun to create a new noun. This isn’t quite the same as a noun with a modifying adjective. We just use a space between the adjective and the noun, so sometimes it can be hard to identify as a compound; however, if the two words are commonly used together, it’s considered to be a compound word.

Example:

Ice cream
Grand jury
Cave in
Post office
Real estate
Middle class
Full moon
Living room
Half sister
Coffee Mug

2. Closed compound words - these words weren’t used together, but they’re now accepted as a “real word” in the English language. Closed compound words are usually made up of only two words. Here are some closed compound examples.

3. Hyphenated compound word - two words which are put together and separated by a hyphen. An example of this type of compound word is ’empty-handed.’

Example:
